  • EMBROIDERY

    • Embroidery is a decorating method that uses needles and vibrant thread to create simple or intricate designs.
    Embroidery is durable and elegant, has almost no effect on washing and can maintain their fine and beautiful look after several years. We use quality polyester and rayon threads for their sheen and strength. We also use high-visibility, metallic and fire-retardant to meet your needs. We employ different embroidery techniques to bring designs to life: A. Flat Direct Embroidery: It’s our most popular embroidery decoration where stitches are applied directly to a garment or fabric creating a flat design. B. 3D Puff Embroidery: It is a technique where foam is inserted under the top stitches to raise the surface, giving a 3-dimensional look to the design (most commonly used on caps). C. Embroidered Patches: A piece of fabric, most frequently cotton twill, is used as backing and the design is stitched directly into it and finished with an embroidered border. There are various ways of affixing them to products such as with a pin, sewn on, iron-on and Velcro backing. D. Tackle Twill or Applique: This technique involves sewing down a cut-out shape of material, typically numbers or letters, onto another material. The edge can then be finished with an embroidered border to create a nice, finished look without adding too many stitches, which can be more expensive. This technique is great for covering large areas with a logo or text where embroidering would be too heavy or make the garment uncomfortable to wear. To keep embroidery from getting damaged, we recommend spot cleaning with warm water and mild detergent, avoid scrubbing the embroidery directly. For machine washing, turn inside out, opt for cold water, mild detergent and run in delicate cycle. Immediately after washing, place on flat surface to air dry or tumble dry on low heat. Do not iron directly on embroidery, turn inside out and use a damp towel or cloth to protect from heat.

  • HEAT TRANSFERS

    • Heat transfer printing is an embellishment method that requires printing a design on special paper and then transferring onto an item using heat and pressure. It is a cost-effective and simple way to create custom-printed products. Raster images (pixel-based artwork such as JPEG’s, TIF’s, BMP’s or PNG’s) must be first converted into vector images, which allows an image to be scaled or modified without losing quality or resolution. The artwork must then be printed onto special paper and flipped on the horizontal axis which allows the design to be placed oriented the right way on the garment. The artwork is then pressed into fabric for a preset period of time. Heat transfer printing is relatively inexpensive compared to other forms of printing like screen printing or dye-sublimation printing and has a faster turnaround time as there is minimal setup time. Heat transfer vinyl typically lasts up to 50+ wash and dry cycles, or one to two years depending on washing frequency. To prolong their lifespan wash clothes inside out, use warm or cold water and hang to air dry or machine dry on low heat setting. Never directly iron on heat transfer as it could melt or burn.

  • DYE SUBLIMATED CRESTS

    • A dye sublimated crest is a patch that is created using a special type of ink that turns into a gas when heated, and then binds with the fibers of the fabric. The design is first printed onto a special type of paper, then placed onto the fabric, and both are placed into a heat press, resulting in a high-quality, full-color crest that is long-lasting and durable.
